The Hidden Costs of Overbed Tables: A Deep Dive Into Workspace Psychology

The rise of remote work has led many to adopt overbed tables as makeshift workspaces, but this convenient solution comes with significant hidden costs. Beyond the obvious ergonomic challenges and physical health risks, using your bed as an office creates dangerous psychological blurs between work and rest spaces. This practice can disrupt sleep patterns, decrease productivity, and compromise your professional image during video calls. Experts warn against the long-term implications, including poor posture, chronic pain, and disrupted work-life balance. Instead, professionals are urged to create dedicated workspaces, even in small living environments, to maintain boundaries and protect their wellbeing.
