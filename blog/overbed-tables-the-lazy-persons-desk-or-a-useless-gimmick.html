Overbed Tables: The Lazy Person's Desk or a Useless Gimmick?

February 25, 2025 • 5 min read

Look, we need to talk.

Overbed tables have been marketed as the ultimate solution for productivity and convenience. Work from bed! Eat from bed! Basically, never leave bed again!

But let's ask the real question: Are overbed tables actually helping anyone be more productive, or are they just an overpriced excuse to stay horizontal indefinitely?

Spoiler alert: It's mostly the second one.

1. Do You Actually Work Better in Bed, or Is This Just a Productivity Illusion?

You tell yourself, "I'll be so efficient with an overbed table!" But let's get real:

  • • You're not writing the next great novel from your comforter cocoon.
  • • That "just five more minutes" nap? Now it's a two-hour accidental coma.
  • • Every meeting you take from bed is one step closer to fully merging with your mattress.

Sure, in theory, working from bed sounds like a dream. But if you think an overbed table will transform you into a productivity powerhouse, please reconsider.

There's a reason real workspaces don't come with pillows and a weighted blanket.

2. Are Overbed Tables Encouraging Bad Posture and Chronic Pain?

Let's talk about ergonomics. You know, that thing where you're supposed to sit with proper back support, aligned wrists, and good posture?

An overbed table completely ignores that.

  • ✅ Hunched shoulders?
  • ✅ Neck craned at an unnatural angle?
  • ✅ Wrists at weird, carpal-tunnel-inducing angles?

Congratulations! Your new overbed office setup is also the fastest way to ruin your spine.

If you actually care about your health and comfort, consider an adjustable standing desk instead. You know, something designed for human anatomy.

3. Is It Smarter to Just Invest in a Real Ergonomic Workspace Instead?

Let's be honest: The real solution isn't an overbed table. It's getting out of bed.

Here's what a functional adult workspace looks like:

  • ✔ An actual desk
  • ✔ A chair that supports your back
  • ✔ A setup that doesn't actively destroy your posture

Sound crazy? It shouldn't. A proper workspace makes you more productive, keeps your body pain-free, and—get this—helps you separate work from sleep.

Investing in an adjustable standing desk makes way more sense than spending money on a glorified hospital tray.

Final Verdict: Do Overbed Tables Have Any Redeeming Qualities?

Fine. There are a few people who might genuinely need one.

If you're recovering from an injury, dealing with mobility challenges, or actually need a bedside workspace for medical reasons, then sure—an overbed table has its uses.

And if that's the case, this is probably the least terrible one out there: Check it out here.

But if you're just looking for an excuse to turn your bed into a full-time workstation, do yourself a favor and get a standing desk instead.

Because let's be real—your spine, productivity, and dignity will thank you.
