Are Overbed Tables a Waste of Money? Here's What the Reviews Don't Tell You

February 25, 2025 • 5 min read

Overbed tables seem great on paper. You've seen the glowing reviews:

"Game-changer for working from bed!"

"Perfect for meals, laptops, and more!"

"So convenient, I barely leave my bed anymore!"

…Wait. That last one should be a red flag.

Because here's the truth no one tells you: Most overbed tables are wobbly, unstable, and one bad decision away from sending your coffee straight into your laptop.

Before you buy one, let's talk about what the reviews conveniently forget to mention.

1. Wobbly, Unstable, and a Spill Disaster Waiting to Happen

You know what's super fun? Trying to type while your desk shakes like it's powered by a malfunctioning washing machine.

  • • Cheap models wobble with every keystroke.
  • • One accidental bump? Congratulations, your laptop is now airborne.
  • • Trying to drink coffee while using one? Good luck.

Unless you love living life on the edge, a flimsy, shaky surface isn't exactly ideal for working, eating, or… well, anything.

If you insist on getting one, at least pick a model that won't turn your workspace into a carnival ride: Try this one.

2. Low Weight Capacity – Your Laptop Might Be Too Heavy

Think you can stack books, a laptop, and your food tray on an overbed table?

Think again.

  • • Many models can barely handle a laptop before they start sagging.
  • • Some bend or collapse under too much weight.
  • • Others have weak hinges that loosen over time, making the whole thing lopsided.

If your workspace needs a weight limit lower than your daily caffeine intake, you might want to reconsider.

Or, you know, just get a real desk instead. This one actually holds weight without threatening to give up on life.

3. The Wheels Have a Mind of Their Own

You know what's worse than an overbed table that won't move?

An overbed table that won't stop moving.

  • • Some wheels refuse to stay locked, meaning your "desk" drifts away mid-use.
  • • Others roll unevenly and make the whole thing lopsided.
  • • And then there's the worst-case scenario: you try to adjust it, and it rolls out of reach.

Do you really want to play a game of "chase the table" while trying to work?

At least get a model where the wheels don't actively betray you: Check this one out.

4. Horrible Ergonomics – Say Goodbye to Your Back and Wrists

Overbed tables claim to make working from bed comfortable. You know what they actually do?

  • • Force you into a hunched-over position.
  • • Strain your wrists because the angle is never quite right.
  • • Destroy your lower back because there's no lumbar support.

If you're looking for long-term neck pain, an overbed table is perfect.

But if you'd rather keep your spine intact, maybe rethink this whole bed-based productivity setup and grab a standing desk instead.

Final Verdict: Are Overbed Tables a Waste of Money?

If you're recovering from surgery or have mobility challenges, sure—an overbed table makes sense. In that case, at least get one that won't collapse under pressure.

But if you're just buying one to "work from bed," please understand:

Overbed tables are not a productivity hack.

They are flimsy, annoying, and ergonomically awful. Instead of wasting your money on something that will betray you in weeks, consider an adjustable standing desk and work like a person who still has hope.

Your spine, laptop, and general dignity will thank you.
