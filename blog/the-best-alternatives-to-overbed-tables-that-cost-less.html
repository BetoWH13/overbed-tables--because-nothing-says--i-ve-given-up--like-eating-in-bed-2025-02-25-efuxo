The Best Alternatives to Overbed Tables That Cost Less

February 25, 2025 • 5 min read

So, you've found yourself on the brink of buying an overbed table. Maybe you're tired of using your actual table like a functional adult. Maybe you just love the idea of eating dinner two feet from your pillow. Either way, let's pause for a second.

Because, believe it or not, there are better, cheaper, and far less tragic alternatives to overbed tables—ones that don't scream "I've fully committed to never leaving my bed."

Let's dive in.

1. A Regular Table (Yes, That's Still an Option)

Cost: You already own one.

Look, I know this is a wild concept, but hear me out: Tables exist outside of beds. And the best part? They're everywhere. Kitchen tables. Coffee tables. Desks. You might even have one mere feet away from your bed!

I know getting up sounds exhausting, but so is explaining to visitors why your entire life now revolves around a rolling hospital tray.

If your actual table is too far away, maybe the issue isn't a lack of an overbed table—maybe it's time to reevaluate your furniture layout.

2. A Lap Desk – The "I'm Trying" Compromise

Cost: $20-$40

Okay, let's say you absolutely must work or eat in bed (sigh). At least make an effort to avoid committing to the full "rolling cart of shame" experience. A simple lap desk does the same job—without the hospital ward aesthetic.

Plus, it's portable, doesn't require wheels, and doesn't make your bed officially part of your office furniture.

Still want something adjustable? Fine, here's a standing desk that won't trap you in a blanket burrito forever: Check this one out.

3. A Folding TV Tray – Because Nostalgia Costs Less

Cost: $15-$30

You know what's basically the same thing as an overbed table, but without the commitment to lifelong bed-based living? A simple folding TV tray.

Remember when people used to eat on these while watching reruns of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? Those were simpler times. And guess what? They still work.

They're cheap, portable, and don't make it look like you're waiting for a hospital nurse to bring you applesauce.

4. Adjustable Standing Desk – For People Who Still Have Hope

Cost: $100-$200

Okay, so you need an adjustable work surface. That's fair. But does it really need to hover over your bed like a mechanical vulture, waiting for you to spill coffee on your sheets?

Enter: The adjustable standing desk. It moves up and down, lets you stand like a human, and—most importantly—keeps you from merging with your mattress forever.

Seriously, if you're thinking about an overbed table but still have some will to live, this is a better investment.

5. Literally Anything Else

Cost: Varies, but still better than an overbed table.

A clipboard. A stack of books. A tray stolen from a fast-food restaurant. ANYTHING that doesn't make your bedroom feel like it should have a nurse call button.

Because let's be real—if your bed needs its own designated furniture, it might be time to just move into the hospital full-time.

But if you're just looking for an excuse to work, eat, and live from bed forever, let's be real—you don't need an overbed table.
