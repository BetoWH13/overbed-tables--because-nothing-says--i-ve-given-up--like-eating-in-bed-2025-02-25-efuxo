Why You Might NOT Need an Overbed Table After All

February 25, 2025 • 5 min read

So, you're thinking about getting an overbed table. Maybe you believe it'll make your life easier. Maybe you think it's the missing piece of your productivity puzzle. Maybe you're just one step away from giving up entirely.

Now, let's be clear—some people genuinely need an overbed table. If you have medical reasons, mobility limitations, or other physical needs, then yes—overbed tables serve a valuable purpose. And in that case, this one might actually be a game-changer for you.

But if your biggest struggle is just "not wanting to get up," then let's take a moment to examine your life choices.

1. Your Bed Is Not a Home Office

I know, I know—working from bed sounds like a dream. No commute. No dress code. Just you, your laptop, and a questionable level of motivation. But let's be honest:

How much actual work happens when you're wrapped in a blanket burrito?

An overbed table won't magically transform your sleep nest into a Fortune 500 workspace. You'll still be in pajamas. You'll still be scrolling social media. And you'll definitely still be asleep during that Zoom call.

If you're looking for a workspace that doesn't actively encourage naps, maybe try an adjustable standing desk instead. You know, one designed for actual work.

2. Do You Really Want a "Hospital Chic" Aesthetic?

Nothing screams "luxury home design" like a rolling medical-grade table hovering over your sheets. Nothing says, "I'm thriving" quite like furniture you'd find in a recovery ward.

Again—if you need one for medical reasons, that's completely different. But if your only ailment is "I don't want to walk to a real table," you might want to rethink things.

Are you decorating your home… or assembling a makeshift hospital suite? Because if you start with an overbed table, what's next? A bedside IV drip for hydration? Adjustable bed rails for extra security?

If this is the interior design path you're on, fine. Here's a bedside contraption to complete the look.

But if you'd rather maintain some level of style and dignity, perhaps it's time to reevaluate your furniture choices.

3. Overbed Tables Are Just a Gateway to Full-Time Bed Life

You might tell yourself, "I'll only use it for breakfast in bed!" or "It's just for occasional work!" But we all know where this road leads.

First, it's morning coffee in bed. Then, it's "just one episode" of your favorite show. Then, suddenly, it's 4 PM and you haven't moved in six hours, aside from reaching for the snack pile you keep conveniently within arm's reach.

At what point do we stop calling it an "overbed table" and start calling it a life raft for people who refuse to get up?

If you're committed to this path, I won't stop you. Here's one that rolls nicely over your excuses.

But if you still have hope, try setting up an actual workspace with a standing desk. Maybe even—brace yourself—a real chair.

Final Thoughts: Do You Even Need This Thing?

At the end of the day, an overbed table is just a stepping stone to never leaving your sheets again. And sure, that might sound nice now… but talk to me when your legs forget what standing feels like.

Again—if you actually need one due to health reasons, I totally get it. In that case, you should have a good one that makes life easier.

But if your biggest struggle is "getting out of bed is too much work," maybe what you really need is a standing desk and a reality check.

Your call.
