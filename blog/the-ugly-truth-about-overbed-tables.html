The Ugly Truth About Overbed Tables

February 25, 2025 • 5 min read

Ah, the overbed table—a piece of furniture so bleak, so tragically utilitarian, that it practically whispers, "You've given up." A table that doesn't ask, "What are your dreams?" but rather, "Would you like your lukewarm soup next to your existential dread?"

If you're even considering an overbed table, it's time for an intervention. And yet, they continue to exist, rolling into bedrooms, hospitals, and—somehow—home offices. Let's unpack the cold, hard truth.

1. Overbed Tables: The Furniture Equivalent of Sweatpants in Public

You know what a regular table says? "I am a functional adult." You know what an overbed table says? "This is where I'll be, forever."

Sure, it's convenient to roll a desk over your bed, but let's be real—when did "convenience" become code for never leaving your comforter again? Next thing you know, you're microwaving coffee from three days ago because walking to the kitchen is just too much.

But hey, if you insist, here's one that rolls nicely over your regret. Don't say I didn't warn you.

2. Productivity? You Mean Procrastination.

Adjustable standing desks? A symbol of ambition. Overbed tables? A cry for help.

Let's not pretend you're going to "get work done" from bed. You know what you're actually going to do? Binge-watch a crime documentary while your laptop teeters on a flimsy piece of MDF.

Want a desk that doesn't enable your horizontal lifestyle? Maybe consider an adjustable standing desk instead—you know, one that requires you to stand up occasionally.

3. The Inevitable Descent Into Bed-Based Living

First, it's just a harmless breakfast tray. Then, it's a full-on overbed workstation. Next thing you know, you're Googling, "Can you mount a mini-fridge to a bed frame?"

This isn't just a furniture choice—it's a lifestyle choice. And that lifestyle? Tragic.

Final Verdict: Overbed Tables, A Slippery Slope to Nowhere

Look, I get it. The idea of never leaving bed again is tempting. But before you surrender to the lure of rolling furniture, ask yourself:

Do you want a table... or do you want your dignity?

If you still think an overbed table is your destiny, fine. Here's one to complete your transformation. But if there's even a shred of hope left in you, consider a standing desk instead.

Your spine will thank you.
