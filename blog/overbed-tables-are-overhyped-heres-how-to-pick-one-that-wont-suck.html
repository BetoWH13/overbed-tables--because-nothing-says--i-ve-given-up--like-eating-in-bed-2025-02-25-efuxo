Overbed Tables Are Overhyped—Here's How to Pick One That Won't Suck

February 25, 2025 • 6 min read

Ah, the overbed table—the furniture world's way of saying, "You've completely given up on standing." These rolling regret machines have been marketed as "game-changers" for productivity, convenience, and comfort.

But let's be honest—most of them suck. They wobble, they get stuck on carpet, and they make you look like you're waiting for someone to check your vitals.

Still determined to buy one? Fine. Here's how to pick an overbed table that won't completely ruin your life.

1. Avoid the "Flimsy TV Tray on Wheels" Models

Most overbed tables are just a sad plank of MDF balanced on a metal frame with the stability of a newborn giraffe. You breathe on it wrong, and your laptop does a trust fall straight onto your face.

What to Look For Instead:

  • ✔ Sturdy base—one that won't tip over if you gasp shift in bed.
  • ✔ Locking wheels—so you don't spend half your life chasing your own table.
  • ✔ Weight capacity—because some of these things act like a single laptop is too much to handle.

If you absolutely must get one, at least pick one that won't fold under pressure: Try this one.

2. Height Adjustment That Actually Works

Some overbed tables claim they're "adjustable"—but what they don't tell you is that adjusting them requires the arm strength of a professional weightlifter.

If you're going to commit to this bed-based lifestyle, you at least deserve a model that adjusts without requiring a prayer circle.

What to Look For Instead:

  • ✔ Pneumatic or crank-adjustable height—unless you enjoy unscrewing bolts at 2 AM.
  • ✔ A range that actually fits your bed height—because not all beds are the same.

Or, you know, you could just get a standing desk and call it a day.

3. Tilt Features: The Difference Between "Useful" and "Useless"

A lot of these overbed tables come with a "tilt" feature that, in theory, should help you work comfortably. In reality?

Congratulations, your laptop is now a slide.

What to Look For Instead:

  • ✔ Lockable tilt angles—so your coffee cup doesn't experience gravity firsthand.
  • ✔ A ledge to prevent objects from launching into your lap.
  • ✔ Something designed for actual humans—not whatever some factory thought "looked ergonomic."

But let's be real, the best way to avoid bad tilt features is to avoid overbed tables altogether.

4. Something That Doesn't Look Like a Hospital Prop

Overbed tables come in two design styles:

  1. 1️⃣ "Medical Equipment Stolen from a Nursing Home."
  2. 2️⃣ "Sleek, Modern, But Still Kind of Depressing."

For something that's going to occupy prime real estate in your bedroom, do you really want it to scream "My physical therapist recommended this"?

What to Look For Instead:

  • ✔ Wood or sleek finishes—so it doesn't look like it came from a hospital storage closet.
  • ✔ Slim but sturdy design—because if it's going to take up space, it might as well look decent.

Or, you could skip this whole mess and just get a standing desk. Crazy idea, right?

Final Verdict: Overbed Tables Still Suck, But If You Must…

Look, I get it. Maybe you're recovering from surgery. Maybe you have mobility limitations. Or maybe you've just fully committed to working from bed like a modern-day Renaissance thinker.
